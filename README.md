## Conclusion

Cartova has built a reasonably broad and demographically diverse customer base of 11,554 people. The loyalty programme has successfully tiered most of the base into Gold and Silver, and channel preferences are well-documented. However, the business is sitting on a retention emergency: nearly 9 in 10 customers have not transacted in over 90 days, satisfaction is split almost evenly across the spectrum, and the strongest value tier (Platinum) is effectively out of reach for the vast majority of customers. The data suggests a business where acquisition and initial engagement worked, but where long-term experience and relevance have broken down. Cartova is not losing customers dramatically; it is losing them quietly through inactivity.


## Recommendation

1. **Launch an urgent 90-day reactivation campaign.**
The 10,228 at-risk customers are not cold leads; they are lapsed members who already know the brand. Segment this group by loyalty tier and reach each customer through their stated preferred channel. A Gold-tier reactivation offer via their preferred channel (SMS, email, phone, or in-person event) is the highest-ROI action available right now.
2. **Investigate the root cause of disengagement and not just the symptom.**
Since lapsed customers hold good loyalty points, the issue is not the rewards programme. Cartova should conduct qualitative research (exit surveys, NPS follow-up calls with lapsed Gold/Silver customers) to understand what broke the relationship. Was it a product assortment? Pricing? A competitor? Digital experience? The dashboard can tell you who left; only qualitative research can tell you why.
3. **Restructure the loyalty tier programme.**
With Platinum exclusive to incomes above $150K, the aspirational ceiling is irrelevant to 99% of customers. Consider introducing a mid-tier milestone (e.g., a "Gold Plus" or "Elite" band at 7,500–9,000 points) that gives Gold members a visible next step. The goal is to make the next tier feel achievable so customers have a reason to transact again.
4. **Treat the 39% dissatisfied segment as a priority.**
Run a deep-dive into the lapsed customers who also gave negative feedback. This is the highest churn-risk sub-group. Address their concerns before running any reactivation campaign; re-engaging them in an unchanged experience will only produce a second lapse.
5. **Build personalised, channel-native communications.**
Since all four channels (Email, SMS, Phone, In-Person) are equally distributed and none dominates even among specific tiers or age groups, a blanket campaign on any single channel will miss 75% of the audience. Invest in a CRM workflow that routes each communication to the customer's stated preference. This is the clearest, most actionable channel insight the data provides.
6. **Develop a youth acquisition strategy.**
With an average customer age of 46.88, the pipeline of younger customers (18–30) appears thin. Without intervention, the active base will age further. A targeted campaign for younger segments, likely digital-first and SMS-led, would diversify the base and improve long-term brand health.
